A matrix is in **Reduced Row Echelon Form (RREF)** if it satisfies a stricter version of **Row Echelon Form (REF)**.

RREF is the final form obtained after performing **Gauss–Jordan elimination**.

The matrix is simplified so much that the **solutions of the system can be read directly** from it.

# Conditions for RREF

A matrix is in **Reduced Row Echelon Form** if it satisfies all of the following properties.

### 1. All zero rows are at the bottom

Any row containing only zeros must appear below all nonzero rows.

Example:

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 & 3 \\
0 & 1 & 4 \\
0 & 0 & 0
\end{bmatrix}
$$

---

### 2. The first nonzero entry in each row is 1

This entry is called a **pivot** or **leading 1**.

Example:

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 & 3 \\
0 & 1 & 4 \\
0 & 0 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
$$

---

### 3. Each pivot is the only nonzero entry in its column

All entries **above and below the pivot must be zero**.

Example:

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 3 \\
0 & 1 & -2 \\
0 & 0 & 0
\end{bmatrix}
$$

---

### 4. Pivots move to the right as you move down the rows

This keeps the **staircase structure**.

Example:

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 3 \\
0 & 1 & -2 \\
0 & 0 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
$$

---

# Example of a Matrix in RREF

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 4 & | & 2 \\
0 & 1 & -3 & | & 5 \\
0 & 0 & 0 & | & 0
\end{bmatrix}
$$

From this matrix we can directly read:

$$
x_1 + 4x_3 = 2
$$

$$
x_2 - 3x_3 = 5
$$

Here \(x_3\) is a **free variable**.

---

# Example: Converting to RREF

Start with

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 & | & 5 \\
3 & 4 & | & 6
\end{bmatrix}
$$

### Step 1 — Eliminate below the pivot

$$
R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - 3R_1
$$

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 & | & 5 \\
0 & -2 & | & -9
\end{bmatrix}
$$

---

### Step 2 — Make pivot equal to 1

$$
R_2 \leftarrow -\frac{1}{2}R_2
$$

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 & | & 5 \\
0 & 1 & | & 4.5
\end{bmatrix}
$$

---

### Step 3 — Eliminate above the pivot

$$
R_1 \leftarrow R_1 - 2R_2
$$

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & | & -4 \\
0 & 1 & | & 4.5
\end{bmatrix}
$$

This matrix is now in **RREF**.

---

# Why RREF is Important

RREF gives immediate information about a matrix:

- solutions of a system
- rank of the matrix
- pivot columns
- free variables
- null space

---

# REF vs RREF

| Property | REF | RREF |
|------|------|------|
| Staircase structure | Yes | Yes |
| Zeros below pivots | Yes | Yes |
| Zeros above pivots | No | Yes |
| Pivot must be 1 | Not required | Required |

Example REF:

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
2 & 3 & 1 \\
0 & 4 & 2 \\
0 & 0 & 5
\end{bmatrix}
$$

Example RREF:

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 3 \\
0 & 1 & -2 \\
0 & 0 & 0
\end{bmatrix}
$$

---

REF simplifies a matrix.

RREF simplifies it **as much as possible**, so the structure of the solution becomes completely visible.
