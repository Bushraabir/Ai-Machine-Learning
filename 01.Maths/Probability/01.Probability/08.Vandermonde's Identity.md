Vandermonde's Identity relates binomial coefficients as:

>[!important]  $$\binom{m+n}{r} = \sum_{k=0}^{r} \binom{m}{k} \binom{n}{r-k}$$

Where:

- $m, n, r \ge 0$ are integers  
- $\binom{a}{b}$ = number of ways to choose \(b\) objects from \(a\) objects

---

## Intuition

Think of two groups:

1. **Group A** with **m elements**  
2. **Group B** with **n elements**

We want to choose **r elements total** from both groups:


- Choose **k from Group A** → $$ \binom{m}{k} ways  $$ 


- Choose **r−k from Group B** → $$\binom{n}{r-k} ways  $$

Sum over all possible \(k\) to cover **all combinations**.
