
A **Probability Mass Function (PMF)** describes the probability distribution of a **discrete random variable** \(X\). It gives the probability that \(X\) takes a specific value \(x\):

$$
p_X(x) = P(X = x)
$$

### Properties:

1. **Non-negativity:**  
$$
p_X(x) \ge 0 \quad \forall x
$$

2. **Normalization:**  
$$
\sum_{x \in \text{Support}} p_X(x) = 1
$$

3. **Discrete Random Variable:**  
	PMF is only defined for discrete outcomes.

---

## Support

The **support** of a discrete random variable \(X\) is the set of all values with **non-zero probability**:

$$
\text{Support}(X) = \{ x : p_X(x) > 0 \}
$$

---

## Examples

### Example 1: Fair Die

Let \(X\) = outcome of a fair 6-sided die.

\[
p_X(x) =
\begin{cases} 
\frac{1}{6} & x = 1,2,3,4,5,6 \\
0 & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
\]

- Support: \(\{1,2,3,4,5,6\}\)  
- Sum of probabilities: \(6 \times \frac{1}{6} = 1\)

### Example 2: Bernoulli Random Variable

Let \(X \sim \text{Bernoulli}(p)\) (success/failure):

\[
p_X(x) =
\begin{cases}
p & x = 1 \ (\text{success}) \\
1-p & x = 0 \ (\text{failure})
\end{cases}
\]

- Support: \(\{0,1\}\)  
- Sum: \(p + (1-p) = 1\)

---

## Relation to CDF

The **Cumulative Distribution Function (CDF)** \(F_X(x)\) is derived from the PMF:

\[
F_X(x) = P(X \le x) = \sum_{t \le x} p_X(t)
\]
