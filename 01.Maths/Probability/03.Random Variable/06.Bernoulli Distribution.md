The **Bernoulli Distribution** models a single experiment (or trial) that has exactly **two outcomes**: **success** or **failure**.  

---

## 1. Parameter

- \(p\) = probability of success  
- \(X\) = outcome of the trial, where:  
  - \(X = 1\) → success  
  - \(X = 0\) → failure

---

## 2. Probability Mass Function (PMF)

$$
P(X = x) = 
\begin{cases} 
p & \text{if } x = 1 \\
1 - p & \text{if } x = 0
\end{cases}
$$

---

## 3. Support

$$
\text{Support}(X) = \{0, 1\}
$$

---

## 4. Expectation and Variance

$$
E[X] = p
$$ 
$$
\text{Var}(X) = p \cdot (1-p)
$$

---

## 5. Example

- Toss a biased coin with probability of heads \(p = 0.7\)  
- Let \(X\) = 1 if heads, 0 if tails  

$$
P(X=1) = 0.7, \quad P(X=0) = 0.3
$$  
$$
\text{Support}(X) = \{0, 1\}
$$
