
The **Binomial Distribution** models the number of **successes** in a fixed number of **independent Bernoulli trials**, each with the same probability of success.

---

## 1. Parameters

- \(n\) = number of trials  
- \(p\) = probability of success in a single trial  
- \(X\) = number of successes

---

## 2. Probability Mass Function (PMF)

For \(k = 0, 1, 2, \dots, n\):  
$$
P(X = k) = \binom{n}{k} p^k (1-p)^{n-k}
$$
where $$\binom{n}{k} = \frac{n!}{k!(n-k)!}$$

---

## 3. Support

$$
\text{Support}(X) = \{0, 1, 2, \dots, n\}
$$

---

## 4. Expectation and Variance

$$
E[X] = n \cdot p
$$ 
$$
\text{Var}(X) = n \cdot p \cdot (1-p)
$$

---

## 5. Example

- Toss a fair coin \(n = 3\) times (\(p = 0.5\))  
- Let \(X\) = number of heads  
$$
P(X=2) = \binom{3}{2} (0.5)^2 (0.5)^1 = 3 \cdot 0.25 \cdot 0.5 = 0.375
$$ 
$$
\text{Support}(X) = \{0, 1, 2, 3\}
$$
