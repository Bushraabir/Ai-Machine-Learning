The **support** of a random variable \(X\) is the set of all values that \(X\) can take with non-zero probability.

---

## 1. Discrete Random Variable

If \(X\) is discrete:  
$$
\text{Support}(X) = \{x \in \mathbb{R} : P(X = x) > 0\}
$$

**Example:**  
- \(X\) = outcome of a fair die roll  
$$
\text{Support}(X) = \{1,2,3,4,5,6\}
$$

---

## 2. Continuous Random Variable

If \(X\) is continuous:  
$$
\text{Support}(X) = \{x \in \mathbb{R} : f_X(x) > 0\}
$$
where \(f_X(x)\) is the probability density function (PDF) of \(X\).

**Example:**  
- $$X \sim \text{Uniform}(0,1)$$
$$
\text{Support}(X) = [0,1]
$$

---

**Intuition:**  
The support is essentially the **range of values** that the random variable can realistically take.
