The **Hypergeometric Distribution** models the number of **successes in a sample drawn without replacement** from a finite population containing a fixed number of successes and failures.

---

## 1. Parameters

- \(N\) = population size  
- \(K\) = number of successes in the population  
- \(n\) = number of draws (sample size)  
- \(X\) = number of successes in the sample

---

## 2. Probability Mass Function (PMF)

For $x = 0, 1, 2, \dots, \min(K,n)$:  
$$
P(X = x) = \frac{\binom{K}{x} \binom{N-K}{n-x}}{\binom{N}{n}}
$$

- Numerator counts the ways to choose \(x\) successes and \(n-x\) failures.  
- Denominator counts the total ways to draw \(n\) items from \(N\).

---

## 3. Support

$$
\text{Support}(X) = \{ \max(0, n-(N-K)), \dots, \min(K,n) \}
$$

---

## 4. Expectation and Variance

$$
E[X] = n \cdot \frac{K}{N}
$$

$$
\text{Var}(X) = n \cdot \frac{K}{N} \cdot \frac{N-K}{N} \cdot \frac{N-n}{N-1}
$$

---

## 5. Example

- Population: \(N = 10\) balls, \(K = 4\) red, \(6\) blue  
- Draw \(n = 3\) balls without replacement  
- Let \(X\) = number of red balls in the sample  

$$
P(X=2) = \frac{\binom{4}{2} \binom{6}{1}}{\binom{10}{3}} 
= \frac{6 \cdot 6}{120} = 0.3
$$

$$
\text{Support}(X) = \{0, 1, 2, 3\}
$$




>A **Hypergeometric distribution** tells you the probability of getting a certain number of “successes” **when you pick items from a group without putting them back**.
>
> - Key: **No replacement → each pick affects the next**.
