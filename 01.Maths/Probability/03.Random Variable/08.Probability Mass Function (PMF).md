Probability Mass Functions means all the separate probability of each event.


The **Probability Mass Function (PMF)** of a **discrete random variable (RV)** \(X\) is a function that gives the **probability that \(X\) takes a specific value \(x\)**:

$$
p_X(x) = P(X = x)
$$

  - Defined **only for discrete random variables**.  
  - Values must satisfy:  $0 \le p_X(x) \le 1$  for all x 
  - $\sum_x p_X(x) = 1$ ; the sum of  the probabilities of each events become 1.


---

## Properties of PMF

1. **Non-negative:**  
$$
p_X(x) \ge 0 \quad \forall x
$$

2. **Normalization (sum = 1):**  
$$
\sum_{x \in \text{Support}(X)} p_X(x) = 1
$$

3. **Support:**  
$$\text{Support}(X) = \{x : p_X(x) > 0\}$$

---

## Relation to CDF

- The **CDF** \(F_X(x)\) can be obtained from PMF:  
$$
F_X(x) = P(X \le x) = \sum_{t \le x} p_X(t)
$$

- Conversely, PMF can be recovered from CDF:  
$$
p_X(x) = F_X(x) - F_X(x^-)
$$
where \(F_X(x^-)\) is the CDF just before \(x\).

---

## Examples

### Example 1: Fair Coin

Toss a fair coin once. Let \(X = 1\) for heads, \(X = 0\) for tails.  

- PMF:  
$$
p_X(0) = 0.5, \quad p_X(1) = 0.5
$$  
- Support: \(\{0, 1\}\)

### Example 2: Number of Heads in 2 Tosses

Let \(X\) = number of heads in 2 fair coin tosses.  

- PMF:  
$$
p_X(0) = 1/4, \quad p_X(1) = 2/4, \quad p_X(2) = 1/4
$$  
- Support: \(\{0, 1, 2\}\)

---

## Key Insights (Stats 110 perspective)

- PMF fully **describes the distribution of a discrete random variable**.  
- All probabilities for events can be computed as **sums of PMF values**:  
$$
P(a \le X \le b) = \sum_{x = a}^{b} p_X(x)
$$
- PMF is the **discrete analog of the PDF** for continuous variables.
