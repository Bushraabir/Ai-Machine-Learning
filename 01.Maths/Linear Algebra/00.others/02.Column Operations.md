A **column operation** is a manipulation applied to the **columns of a matrix**.

While **row operations change equations**, **column operations change the variables or coordinate directions**.

Column operations are less common in solving systems, but they are important in understanding **matrix structure, transformations, and linear algebra theory**.

---

# The Three Types of Column Operations

## 1. Column Swap

Exchange two columns of a matrix.

$$
C_i \leftrightarrow C_j
$$

Example:

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 \\
3 & 4
\end{bmatrix}
\rightarrow
\begin{bmatrix}
2 & 1 \\
4 & 3
\end{bmatrix}
$$

This swaps the **order of variables** in the system.

---

## 2. Column Scaling

Multiply a column by a nonzero constant.

$$
C_i \leftarrow cC_i
$$

Example:

$$
C_2 \leftarrow 2C_2
$$

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 \\
3 & 4
\end{bmatrix}
\rightarrow
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 4 \\
3 & 8
\end{bmatrix}
$$

This scales the **contribution of that variable**.

---

## 3. Column Replacement

Add a multiple of one column to another column.

$$
C_i \leftarrow C_i + \ell C_j
$$

Example:

$$
C_2 \leftarrow C_2 + C_1
$$

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 \\
3 & 4
\end{bmatrix}
\rightarrow
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 3 \\
3 & 7
\end{bmatrix}
$$

This combines **two column directions**.

---

# Column Operations as Matrix Multiplication

Column operations correspond to multiplying by a matrix on the **right side**.

Key rule:

$$
\text{Right multiplication} \Rightarrow \text{Column operation}
$$

$$
AE
$$

where \(E\) is an **elementary matrix**.

---

# Example

Let

$$
A =
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 \\
3 & 4
\end{bmatrix}
$$

and

$$
E =
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 \\
2 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
$$

Compute

$$
AE
$$

$$
AE =
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 \\
3 & 4
\end{bmatrix}
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 \\
2 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
5 & 2 \\
11 & 4
\end{bmatrix}
$$

This corresponds to the column operation

$$
C_1 \leftarrow C_1 + 2C_2
$$

---

# Interpretation

Remember the key conceptual difference:

- **Row operations modify equations**
- **Column operations modify variables**

If we write

$$
Ax = b
$$

then:

- Row operations transform the **system of equations**
- Column operations transform the **coordinate system of the variables**

---

# Geometric Meaning

Columns of a matrix represent **directions in space**.

A column operation changes those directions.

For example:

$$
A =
\begin{bmatrix}
| & | \\
c_1 & c_2 \\
| & |
\end{bmatrix}
$$

After a column operation:

$$
C_2 \leftarrow C_2 + C_1
$$

the second direction becomes

$$
c_2 + c_1
$$

So the **basis directions change**.

---

# When Column Operations Are Used

Column operations appear in:

- **Basis changes**
- **Coordinate transformations**
- **Matrix factorizations**
- **Understanding column space**
- **Linear transformations**

However, they are **not typically used to solve \(Ax=b\)**.

For solving systems, **row operations are the standard tool**.

---

# Core Rule

$$
\text{Right multiply} \Rightarrow \text{Column operation}
$$

$$
AE
$$

Row operations change **equations**.  
Column operations change **variables**.
