A **row operation** is a basic manipulation applied to the **rows of a matrix**.  
These operations are used to simplify matrices, especially when solving systems of linear equations using **Gaussian elimination**.

The key property:

> Row operations **do not change the solution** of the system \(Ax = b\).

They only rewrite the equations in a simpler form.

# The Three Types of Row Operations

## 1. Row Swap

Exchange two rows of a matrix.

$$
R_i \leftrightarrow R_j
$$

Example:

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 \\
3 & 4
\end{bmatrix}
\rightarrow
\begin{bmatrix}
3 & 4 \\
1 & 2
\end{bmatrix}
$$

Use this when the pivot element is **zero** and you need a nonzero element.

---

## 2. Row Scaling

Multiply a row by a **nonzero constant**.

$$
R_i \leftarrow cR_i
$$

Example:

$$
R_2 \leftarrow 2R_2
$$

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 \\
3 & 4
\end{bmatrix}
\rightarrow
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 \\
6 & 8
\end{bmatrix}
$$

This operation is often used to **create pivot = 1**.

---

## 3. Row Replacement

Add a multiple of one row to another row.

$$
R_i \leftarrow R_i + \ell R_j
$$

Example:

$$
R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - 3R_1
$$

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 \\
3 & 4
\end{bmatrix}
\rightarrow
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 \\
0 & -2
\end{bmatrix}
$$

This is the **core operation of Gaussian elimination**, used to eliminate entries below a pivot.

---

# Why Row Operations Work

When solving

$$
Ax = b
$$

each row represents an equation.

Row operations simply **rewrite the equations** without changing their solutions.

For example:

$$
x + y = 2
$$

$$
x - y = 0
$$

If we replace

$$
R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - R_1
$$

we get

$$
x + y = 2
$$

$$
-2y = -2
$$

The **system changed form**, but the **solution stayed the same**.

---

# Row Operations as Matrix Multiplication

Every row operation corresponds to multiplying by an **elementary matrix**.

Important rule:

$$
\text{Row operations are performed by multiplying on the left}
$$

$$
EA
$$

Example:

Let

$$
A =
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 \\
3 & 4
\end{bmatrix}
$$

and

$$
E =
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 \\
2 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
$$

Then

$$
EA =
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 \\
5 & 8
\end{bmatrix}
$$

This corresponds to the row operation

$$
R_2 \leftarrow R_2 + 2R_1
$$

---

# When Row Operations Are Used

Row operations are used in:

- **Gaussian Elimination**
- **Finding the inverse of a matrix**
- **Computing matrix rank**
- **Finding the null space**
- **Solving systems of linear equations**
- **Reducing matrices to row echelon form**

---

# Key Idea to Remember

Row operations change the **rows (equations)** but preserve the **solution set**.

In matrix form:

$$
Ax = b
$$

Row operations transform the system into a simpler one:

$$
Ux = c
$$

where \(U\) is an **upper triangular matrix**.

This makes the system easy to solve by **back substitution**.

---

# Core Rule

$$
\text{Left multiplication} \Rightarrow \text{Row operation}
$$

$$
EA
$$

Row operations modify **equations**, not the unknown variables.
