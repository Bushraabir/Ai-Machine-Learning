A matrix is in **Row Echelon Form (REF)** if it has a staircase-like structure created using **elementary row operations**.

REF is the form typically obtained after performing **Gaussian elimination**.

---

# Conditions for Row Echelon Form

A matrix is in REF if it satisfies the following properties.

### 1. All nonzero rows are above any zero rows

Rows containing only zeros must appear at the **bottom** of the matrix.

Example:

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 & 3 \\
0 & 4 & 5 \\
0 & 0 & 0
\end{bmatrix}
$$

---

### 2. Each leading entry (pivot) is to the right of the pivot above it

This creates a **staircase pattern**.

Example:

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 & 3 \\
0 & 4 & 5 \\
0 & 0 & 6
\end{bmatrix}
$$

Pivot positions:

- Row 1 → column 1  
- Row 2 → column 2  
- Row 3 → column 3

---

### 3. All entries below each pivot are zero

Each pivot clears the column below it.

Example:

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 & 3 \\
0 & 4 & 5 \\
0 & 0 & 6
\end{bmatrix}
$$

---

# What is a Pivot?

A **pivot** is the **first nonzero entry of a row**.

Example:

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
2 & 4 & 6 \\
0 & 3 & 1 \\
0 & 0 & 5
\end{bmatrix}
$$

Pivots:

- \(2\) in column 1  
- \(3\) in column 2  
- \(5\) in column 3

---

# Example: Converting to REF

Start with

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 & 1 \\
2 & 4 & 3 \\
3 & 6 & 5
\end{bmatrix}
$$

### Step 1 — Eliminate below the first pivot

$$
R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - 2R_1
$$

$$
R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - 3R_1
$$

Result:

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 & 1 \\
0 & 0 & 1 \\
0 & 0 & 2
\end{bmatrix}
$$

---

### Step 2 — Eliminate below the next pivot

$$
R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - 2R_2
$$

Result:

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 & 1 \\
0 & 0 & 1 \\
0 & 0 & 0
\end{bmatrix}
$$

Now the matrix is in **Row Echelon Form**.

---

# Why REF is Useful

REF makes solving systems easy.

If we have

$$
Ax = b
$$

after Gaussian elimination we get

$$
Ux = c
$$

where \(U\) is an **upper triangular matrix**.

Then we solve using **back substitution**.

---

# REF vs RREF

| Property | REF | RREF |
|------|------|------|
| Staircase structure | Yes | Yes |
| Zeros below pivots | Yes | Yes |
| Zeros above pivots | No | Yes |
| Pivot must be 1 | Not required | Required |

Example REF:

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
2 & 3 & 1 \\
0 & 4 & 2 \\
0 & 0 & 5
\end{bmatrix}
$$

Example RREF:

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 3 \\
0 & 1 & -2 \\
0 & 0 & 0
\end{bmatrix}
$$
---

REF represents the **partially simplified structure** of a matrix produced by **Gaussian elimination**.

It reveals:

- pivot columns  
- rank of the matrix  
- how many independent equations exist
