**Gauss–Jordan elimination** is a systematic method for transforming a matrix into **Reduced Row Echelon Form (RREF)** using **elementary row operations**.

The purpose is usually to:
- solve a system of linear equations
- compute a matrix inverse
- find the rank of a matrix
- determine the null space

Mathematically, the goal is to transform $[A \mid b]$ into $[I \mid x]$ where $I$ is the identity matrix and $x$ is the solution vector.

* [[02.Gaussian Elimination]] stops when the matrix is **upper triangular**.
* Gauss–Jordan elimination goes **one step further** and eliminates entries **above the pivots as well**, producing [[04.Reduced Row Echelon Form (RREF)]]|**Reduced Row Echelon Form**.
]]
# Allowed Row Operations

Only three operations are allowed.
### 1. Row Swap
$R_i \leftrightarrow R_j$ : Swap two rows.
### 2. Row Scaling
$R_i \leftarrow cR_i \quad (c \neq 0)$: Multiply a row by a non-zero constant.
### 3. Row Replacement
$R_i \leftarrow R_i + \ell R_j$: Add a multiple of one row to another.
# Goal: Reduced Row Echelon Form (RREF)

A matrix is in **RREF** if:
1. Each pivot is **1**
2. Each pivot is the **only non-zero number in its column**
3. Pivots move **to the right as you go down rows**
4. Rows of zeros (if any) are at the **bottom**
Example of RREF: $\begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & 3 \\0 & 1 & -2 \\0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}$

# Steps of Gauss–Jordan Elimination

#### 1) Form the Augmented Matrix
Write the system $Ax = b$ as $[A \mid b]$ .Example: $\begin{cases}2x - y = 0 \\-x + 2y = 3\end{cases}$ becomes $\begin{bmatrix}2 & -1 & | & 0 \\-1 & 2 & | & 3\end{bmatrix}$
#### 2) Create the First Pivot
Make the first non-zero element in the first column equal to **1**.Divide the row if necessary.
#### 3)Eliminate Entries Below the Pivot
Use row replacement to make all numbers **below the pivot equal to 0**.This is the same step used in **Gaussian elimination**.
#### 4) Move to the Next Column
Find the next pivot in the next column and repeat the elimination process.Continue until the matrix is **upper triangular**.
#### 5) Eliminate Above the Pivots
This is the extra step that makes Gauss–Jordan different.Use row operations to eliminate all numbers **above each pivot**.
Example pivot column:$\begin{bmatrix}0 \\1 \\0\end{bmatrix}$
#### 6) Ensure Every Pivot is 1

If necessary, scale rows so each pivot equals **1**.Now the matrix is in **Reduced Row Echelon Form**.

## Example:

Start with $\begin{bmatrix}1 & 2 & | & 5 \\3 & 4 & | & 6\end{bmatrix}$ Eliminate below pivot: $R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - 3R_1$
$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 & | & 5 \\
0 & -2 & | & -9
\end{bmatrix}
$$

Make pivot \(1\): $R_2 \leftarrow -\frac{1}{2}R_2$
$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 & | & 5 \\
0 & 1 & | & 4.5
\end{bmatrix}
$$
Eliminate above pivot: $R_1 \leftarrow R_1 - 2R_2$
$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & | & -4 \\
0 & 1 & | & 4.5
\end{bmatrix}
$$
Solution: $x = -4, \quad y = 4.5$
### Gaussian vs Gauss–Jordan

| Method                          | Final Matrix             | Solve Method      |
| ------------------------------- | ------------------------ | ----------------- |
| [[02.Gaussian Elimination]]     | Upper triangular         | Back substitution |
| [[06.Gauss–Jordan Elimination]] | Reduced Row Echelon Form | Solution directly |

### Key Insight:
- Gaussian elimination simplifies the system.
- Gauss–Jordan elimination **completely solves the system inside the matrix**.
- The final matrix directly reveals the solution.
- Every row operation is **left-multiplication by an elementary matrix** $E$.

### Type 1: Row Swap
Swap rows $i$ and $j$.Example (swap $R_1$ and $R_2$): $E =\begin{pmatrix}0 & 1 & 0 \\1 & 0 & 0 \\0 & 0 & 1\end{pmatrix}$
- $E^{-1} = E$ (self-inverse)
### Type 2: Row Scaling
Multiply row $i$ by $c \neq 0$.Example (scale $R_2$ by $c$): $E =\begin{pmatrix}1 & 0 & 0 \\0 & c & 0 \\0 & 0 & 1\end{pmatrix}$
- $E^{-1}$ replaces $c$ with $c^{-1}$
### Type 3: Row Replacement
$R_i \leftarrow R_i + c R_j$ Example (add $cR_2$ to $R_1$): $E =\begin{pmatrix}1 & c & 0 \\0 & 1 & 0 \\0 & 0 & 1\end{pmatrix}$
- $E^{-1}$ uses $-c$


### Key Points:

All elementary matrices are invertible ⇒ row operations:

- Preserve solution sets of $Ax = b$
- Preserve rank and null space
- Do **not** preserve column space
