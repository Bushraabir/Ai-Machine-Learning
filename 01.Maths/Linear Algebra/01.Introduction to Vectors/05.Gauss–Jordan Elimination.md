*Gaussian elimination gets you to row echelon form (REF). Gauss–Jordan continues to reduced row echelon form (RREF). The extra work gives you the inverse directly and explicit solutions without back-substitution.*

---

## Elementary Row Operations and Their Matrices

Every row operation is **left-multiplication by an elementary matrix** $E$.

### Type 1: Row Swap

Swap rows $i$ and $j$.

Example (swap $R_1$ and $R_2$):

$$
E =
\begin{pmatrix}
0 & 1 & 0 \\
1 & 0 & 0 \\
0 & 0 & 1
\end{pmatrix}
$$

- $E^{-1} = E$ (self-inverse)

---

### Type 2: Row Scaling

Multiply row $i$ by $c \neq 0$.

Example (scale $R_2$ by $c$):

$$
E =
\begin{pmatrix}
1 & 0 & 0 \\
0 & c & 0 \\
0 & 0 & 1
\end{pmatrix}
$$

- $E^{-1}$ replaces $c$ with $c^{-1}$

---

### Type 3: Row Replacement

$R_i \leftarrow R_i + c R_j$

Example (add $cR_2$ to $R_1$):

$$
E =
\begin{pmatrix}
1 & c & 0 \\
0 & 1 & 0 \\
0 & 0 & 1
\end{pmatrix}
$$

- $E^{-1}$ uses $-c$

---

### Key Fact

All elementary matrices are invertible ⇒ row operations:

- Preserve solution sets of $Ax = b$
- Preserve rank and null space
- Do **not** preserve column space

---

## Gaussian Elimination (Forward Phase)

Goal: reduce to **Row Echelon Form (REF)**

### REF Conditions

1. Zero rows at bottom  
2. Pivots move right as you go down  
3. Entries below pivots are zero  

---

## Worked Example (3×3 System)

$$
A =
\begin{pmatrix}
2 & 1 & -1 \\
-3 & -1 & 2 \\
-2 & 1 & 2
\end{pmatrix},
\quad
\mathbf{b} =
\begin{pmatrix}
8 \\
-11 \\
-3
\end{pmatrix}
$$

Augmented matrix:

$$
\left(
\begin{array}{ccc|c}
2 & 1 & -1 & 8 \\
-3 & -1 & 2 & -11 \\
-2 & 1 & 2 & -3
\end{array}
\right)
$$

---

### Step 1: Eliminate Below First Pivot

$$
R_2 \leftarrow R_2 + \frac{3}{2}R_1
\quad
R_3 \leftarrow R_3 + R_1
$$

Result:

$$
\left(
\begin{array}{ccc|c}
2 & 1 & -1 & 8 \\
0 & \tfrac{1}{2} & \tfrac{1}{2} & 1 \\
0 & 2 & 1 & 5
\end{array}
\right)
$$

---

### Step 2: Eliminate Below Second Pivot

$$
R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - 4R_2
$$

Result (REF):

$$
\left(
\begin{array}{ccc|c}
2 & 1 & -1 & 8 \\
0 & \tfrac{1}{2} & \tfrac{1}{2} & 1 \\
0 & 0 & -1 & 1
\end{array}
\right)
$$

---

## Gauss–Jordan (Backward Phase)

Goal: reach **RREF**

### RREF Conditions

4. Pivot = 1  
5. Zeros above pivots  

---

### Step 3: Normalize Third Pivot

$$
R_3 \leftarrow -R_3
$$

$$
\left(
\begin{array}{ccc|c}
2 & 1 & -1 & 8 \\
0 & \tfrac{1}{2} & \tfrac{1}{2} & 1 \\
0 & 0 & 1 & -1
\end{array}
\right)
$$

---

### Step 4: Eliminate Above Pivot (Column 3)

$$
R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - \tfrac{1}{2}R_3
\quad
R_1 \leftarrow R_1 + R_3
$$

$$
\left(
\begin{array}{ccc|c}
2 & 1 & 0 & 7 \\
0 & \tfrac{1}{2} & 0 & \tfrac{3}{2} \\
0 & 0 & 1 & -1
\end{array}
\right)
$$

---

### Step 5: Normalize Second Pivot

$$
R_2 \leftarrow 2R_2
$$

$$
\left(
\begin{array}{ccc|c}
2 & 1 & 0 & 7 \\
0 & 1 & 0 & 3 \\
0 & 0 & 1 & -1
\end{array}
\right)
$$

---

### Step 6: Eliminate Above Pivot (Column 2)

$$
R_1 \leftarrow R_1 - R_2
$$

$$
\left(
\begin{array}{ccc|c}
2 & 0 & 0 & 4 \\
0 & 1 & 0 & 3 \\
0 & 0 & 1 & -1
\end{array}
\right)
$$

---

### Step 7: Normalize First Pivot

$$
R_1 \leftarrow \tfrac{1}{2}R_1
$$

Final RREF:

$$
\left(
\begin{array}{ccc|c}
1 & 0 & 0 & 2 \\
0 & 1 & 0 & 3 \\
0 & 0 & 1 & -1
\end{array}
\right)
$$

---

## Solution

$$
x_1 = 2, \quad x_2 = 3, \quad x_3 = -1
$$

---

## Computing $A^{-1}$

Augment with identity:

$$
[A \mid I] \rightarrow [I \mid A^{-1}]
$$

### Example Setup

$$
\left(
\begin{array}{ccc|ccc}
a_{11} & a_{12} & a_{13} & 1 & 0 & 0 \\
a_{21} & a_{22} & a_{23} & 0 & 1 & 0 \\
a_{31} & a_{32} & a_{33} & 0 & 0 & 1
\end{array}
\right)
$$

After full reduction:

$$
\left(
\begin{array}{ccc|ccc}
1 & 0 & 0 & * & * & * \\
0 & 1 & 0 & * & * & * \\
0 & 0 & 1 & * & * & *
\end{array}
\right)
$$

Right side = $A^{-1}$

---

## Algebraic View

Row operations = multiplication:

$$
E_p \cdots E_1 A = R
$$

- If $A$ invertible → $R = I$ → product = $A^{-1}$
- Otherwise → RREF gives structure of solutions

---

## Rank and Solutions

| Case | Meaning |
|------|--------|
| Full rank | Unique solution |
| Free variables | Infinite solutions |
| Inconsistent row | No solution |

---

## Complexity

- REF: $\frac{2}{3}n^3$
- Full Gauss–Jordan: $\sim n^3$

LU decomposition is usually preferred computationally.

---

## Key Takeaways

- RREF is **unique**
- Row operations preserve:
  - Rank
  - Null space
- They do **not** preserve column space
- Gauss–Jordan = elimination + inversion machinery
