## 1. Standard Forms (Vertex at Origin)

|Orientation|Equation|Focus|Directrix|Axis|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|Opens **up**|$x^2 = 4py,\ p>0$|$(0,,p)$|$y = -p$|$x = 0$|
|Opens **down**|$x^2 = -4py,\ p>0$|$(0,,-p)$|$y = p$|$x = 0$|
|Opens **right**|$y^2 = 4px,\ p>0$|$(p,,0)$|$x = -p$|$y = 0$|
|Opens **left**|$y^2 = -4px,\ p>0$|$(-p,,0)$|$x = p$|$y = 0$|

## 2. Standard Forms (Vertex at $(h,k)$)

|Orientation|Equation|
|---|---|
|Opens **up**|$(x-h)^2 = 4p(y-k)$|
|Opens **down**|$(x-h)^2 = -4p(y-k)$|
|Opens **right**|$(y-k)^2 = 4p(x-h)$|
|Opens **left**|$(y-k)^2 = -4p(x-h)$|

**For all cases:** $$\text{Focus} = \text{vertex} + p\cdot(\text{unit vector along axis, toward opening})$$ $$\text{Vertex} = \text{midpoint of (focus, foot on directrix)}$$

---

## 3. Slope–Intercept ↔ Standard Form

$$y = ax^2 + bx + c \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad (x-h)^2 = \frac{1}{a}(y-k) \cdot \text{sign}(a) \cdot 4p$$

More directly:

$$y = a(x-h)^2 + k \quad \Longrightarrow \quad 4p = \frac{1}{|a|}, \quad p = \frac{1}{4|a|}$$

- $a > 0$: opens up; $\quad a < 0$: opens down
- Vertex: $(h,,k)$
- Focus: $\left(h,;k + \dfrac{1}{4a}\right)$
- Directrix: $y = k - \dfrac{1}{4a}$

---

## 4. Key Measurements

$$\text{Focal length (vertex to focus)} = p$$

$$\text{Latus rectum length} = 4p$$

$$\text{Focal chord length through focus at angle } \theta = \frac{4p}{\sin^2\theta}$$

$$\text{Eccentricity} = e = 1$$

---

## 5. Tangent and Normal Lines

For $x^2 = 4py$ at point $P_0 = (x_0, y_0)$:

$$\text{Tangent:} \quad xx_0 = 2p(y + y_0)$$

$$\text{Slope of tangent:} \quad m = \frac{x_0}{2p}$$

$$\text{Normal:} \quad y - y_0 = -\frac{2p}{x_0}(x - x_0)$$

**Tangent in slope form** (tangent with slope $m$):

$$y = mx - pm^2$$

_(Touches $x^2 = 4py$ at $(2pm,, pm^2)$)_

---

## 6. Parametric Equations

For $x^2 = 4py$:

$$x = 2pt, \quad y = pt^2, \quad t \in \mathbb{R}$$

For $y^2 = 4px$:

$$x = pt^2, \quad y = 2pt, \quad t \in \mathbb{R}$$

Tangent at parameter $t$:

$$ty = x + pt^2 \quad \text{(for } y^2 = 4px\text{)}$$

---

## 7. Curvature

$$\kappa(t) = \frac{1}{2p(1+t^2)^{3/2}}$$

$$\kappa\big|_{\text{vertex}} = \frac{1}{2p} \quad \Longrightarrow \quad \text{Radius of curvature at vertex} = 2p$$

_(The osculating circle at the vertex has diameter equal to the latus rectum.)_

---

## 8. Arc Length

For $y = \frac{x^2}{4p}$ from $x = 0$ to $x = a$:

$$L = \int_0^a \sqrt{1 + \frac{x^2}{4p^2}},dx = \frac{a}{2}\sqrt{1+\frac{a^2}{4p^2}} + p\ln!\left(\frac{a}{2p} + \sqrt{1+\frac{a^2}{4p^2}}\right)$$

---

## 9. Area Under a Parabolic Arc

Area between $y = ax^2$ and the horizontal line $y = h$ (archimedes):

$$A = \frac{4}{3} \cdot (\text{base}) \cdot (\text{height}) \cdot \frac{1}{2} = \frac{2}{3},bh$$

where $b = 2\sqrt{h/a}$ is the base width. _(Archimedes' result: area $= \tfrac{2}{3}$ of the circumscribed rectangle.)_

General area between $y = ax^2 + bx + c$ and $y = dx + e$ (intersecting at $x_1, x_2$):

$$A = \frac{|a|}{6}(x_2 - x_1)^3$$

---

## 10. Reflective (Optical) Property

Any ray **parallel to the axis** reflects through the **focus**.

Any ray **through the focus** reflects **parallel to the axis**.

Formally, at point $P_0$ on the parabola: $$\angle(\text{incoming ray}, \text{normal}) = \angle(\text{normal}, \overrightarrow{P_0F})$$

---

## 11. Polar Form

With focus at the pole, directrix perpendicular to polar axis:

$$r = \frac{ed}{1 - e\cos\theta} \quad (e=1, \text{ directrix left of focus})$$

$$r = \frac{p}{1 - \cos\theta} \quad \text{(standard: directrix } x = -p\text{)}$$

All four orientations:

|Directrix|Equation|
|---|---|
|$x = -p$ (left)|$r = \dfrac{p}{1 - \cos\theta}$|
|$x = p$ (right)|$r = \dfrac{p}{1 + \cos\theta}$|
|$y = -p$ (below)|$r = \dfrac{p}{1 - \sin\theta}$|
|$y = p$ (above)|$r = \dfrac{p}{1 + \sin\theta}$|

---

## 12. Discriminant Condition (General Conic)

$$Ax^2 + Bxy + Cy^2 + Dx + Ey + F = 0 \text{ is a parabola} \iff B^2 - 4AC = 0$$

---

## 13. Quick Identification Cheat Sheet

Given $y = ax^2 + bx + c$:

$$\text{Vertex: } \left(-\frac{b}{2a},; c - \frac{b^2}{4a}\right)$$

$$\text{Focus: } \left(-\frac{b}{2a},; c - \frac{b^2-1}{4a}\right) = \left(-\frac{b}{2a},; \frac{4ac - b^2 + 1}{4a}\right)$$

$$\text{Directrix: } y = c - \frac{b^2+1}{4a}$$

$$\text{Axis of symmetry: } x = -\frac{b}{2a}$$

$$p = \frac{1}{4|a|}$$
