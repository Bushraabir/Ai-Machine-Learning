

> _Every formula here traces back to one identity: $c^2 = a^2 + b^2$ — the sign flip that opens the ellipse into two infinite branches._

---

## 1. Fundamental Relation

$$\boxed{c^2 = a^2 + b^2}$$

|Symbol|Meaning|
|---|---|
|$a$|semi-transverse axis|
|$b$|semi-conjugate axis|
|$c$|center-to-focus distance ($c > a$)|
|$e = c/a$|eccentricity, $e > 1$|

**Contrast with ellipse:** $c^2 = a^2 - b^2$ (ellipse) vs $c^2 = a^2 + b^2$ (hyperbola).

---

## 2. Standard Forms (Center at Origin)

**Horizontal transverse axis** (opens left/right):

$$\frac{x^2}{a^2} - \frac{y^2}{b^2} = 1$$

**Vertical transverse axis** (opens up/down):

$$\frac{y^2}{a^2} - \frac{x^2}{b^2} = 1$$

**Rule:** The **positive** term's variable tells you the direction the hyperbola opens.

---

## 3. Standard Forms (Center at $(h, k)$)

|Opens|Equation|
|---|---|
|Left/Right|$\dfrac{(x-h)^2}{a^2} - \dfrac{(y-k)^2}{b^2} = 1$|
|Up/Down|$\dfrac{(y-k)^2}{a^2} - \dfrac{(x-h)^2}{b^2} = 1$|

---

## 4. Key Points (Horizontal Transverse Axis)

|Feature|Coordinates|
|---|---|
|Center|$(h,\ k)$|
|Vertices|$(h \pm a,\ k)$|
|Foci|$(h \pm c,\ k)$, $c = \sqrt{a^2+b^2}$|
|Ends of latus rectum|$\left(h \pm c,\ k \pm \dfrac{b^2}{a}\right)$|
|Directrices|$x = h \pm \dfrac{a}{e} = h \pm \dfrac{a^2}{c}$|
|Asymptotes|$y - k = \pm\dfrac{b}{a}(x - h)$|

_(For vertical: swap $x \leftrightarrow y$ roles; asymptote slopes become $\pm a/b$.)_

---

## 5. Eccentricity

$$e = \frac{c}{a} = \frac{\sqrt{a^2+b^2}}{a} = \sqrt{1 + \frac{b^2}{a^2}} > 1$$

$$c = ae, \qquad b^2 = a^2(e^2-1), \qquad b = a\sqrt{e^2-1}$$

|$e$|Shape|
|---|---|
|$e \to 1^+$|Narrow, nearly parabolic|
|$e = \sqrt{2}$|Rectangular ($a=b$, asymptotes $\perp$)|
|$e \to \infty$|Wide open branches|

---

## 6. Asymptotes

For $\dfrac{x^2}{a^2} - \dfrac{y^2}{b^2} = 1$ (center at origin):

$$y = \pm\frac{b}{a}x$$

For $\dfrac{y^2}{a^2} - \dfrac{x^2}{b^2} = 1$:

$$y = \pm\frac{a}{b}x$$

**Compact form** — asymptotes are the equation with RHS $= 0$:

$$\frac{x^2}{a^2} - \frac{y^2}{b^2} = 0 \implies \left(\frac{x}{a}-\frac{y}{b}\right)\left(\frac{x}{a}+\frac{y}{b}\right) = 0$$

**Asymptote box:** Draw rectangle with half-sides $a$ (horizontal) and $b$ (vertical); diagonals are the asymptotes.

**Angle between asymptotes:**

$$\tan\theta = \frac{2ab}{|a^2 - b^2|} \quad (a \neq b)$$

For rectangular hyperbola ($a = b$): $\theta = 90°$.

---

## 7. Latus Rectum

$$\text{Length of latus rectum} = \frac{2b^2}{a}$$

$$\text{Semi-latus rectum} = \ell = \frac{b^2}{a}$$

_(Chord through focus, perpendicular to transverse axis. Same formula as the ellipse.)_

---

## 8. Focal Radii

For a point $P = (x_0, y_0)$ on the **right branch** of $\dfrac{x^2}{a^2} - \dfrac{y^2}{b^2} = 1$:

$$|PF_1| = |ex_0 + a| = ex_0 + a, \qquad |PF_2| = |ex_0 - a| = ex_0 - a$$

$$|PF_1| - |PF_2| = 2a \quad \checkmark$$

For the **left branch** ($x_0 < 0$):

$$|PF_2| - |PF_1| = 2a$$

---

## 9. Focus-Directrix Property

$$\frac{|PF|}{d(P,,\ell)} = e > 1 \quad \text{for every } P \text{ on the hyperbola}$$

Directrix location (horizontal transverse axis):

$$x = \pm\frac{a}{e} = \pm\frac{a^2}{c}$$

_(Directrices lie between the two vertices — inside the hyperbola.)_

---

## 10. Tangent and Normal Lines

At point $(x_0, y_0)$ on $\dfrac{x^2}{a^2} - \dfrac{y^2}{b^2} = 1$:

$$\text{Tangent:} \quad \frac{x,x_0}{a^2} - \frac{y,y_0}{b^2} = 1$$

$$\text{Slope of tangent:} \quad m = \frac{b^2 x_0}{a^2 y_0}$$

$$\text{Normal:} \quad \frac{a^2 x}{x_0} + \frac{b^2 y}{y_0} = a^2 + b^2$$

**Tangent with slope $m$** (requires $|m| < b/a$ for horizontal, $|m| > b/a$ for vertical):

$$y = mx \pm \sqrt{a^2m^2 - b^2}$$

_(Condition for tangency: $a^2m^2 - b^2 > 0$, i.e., slope must be steeper than asymptotes.)_

---

## 11. Parametric Equations

**Hyperbolic parametrization** (right branch):

$$x = a\cosh t, \quad y = b\sinh t, \quad t \in \mathbb{R}$$

Left branch: $x = -a\cosh t$, $y = b\sinh t$.

**Trigonometric parametrization** (both branches):

$$x = a\sec t, \quad y = b\tan t$$

$$t \in \left(-\tfrac{\pi}{2}, \tfrac{\pi}{2}\right) \text{ (right branch)}, \quad t \in \left(\tfrac{\pi}{2}, \tfrac{3\pi}{2}\right) \text{ (left branch)}$$

**Tangent at parameter $t$** (hyperbolic):

$$\frac{x\cosh t}{a} - \frac{y\sinh t}{b} = 1$$

---

## 12. Polar Form (Focus at Pole)

$$r = \frac{\ell}{1 - e\cos\theta}, \quad \ell = \frac{b^2}{a}, \quad e > 1$$

|$\theta$|Branch|
|---|---|
|$\theta = \pi$|Near branch: $r = \dfrac{\ell}{1+e}$|
|$\theta = 0$|Far branch: $r = \dfrac{\ell}{1-e} < 0$|
|$\theta = \pi/2$|$r = \ell$|

All four orientations:

|Opening|Polar equation|
|---|---|
|Right|$r = \dfrac{\ell}{1 - e\cos\theta}$|
|Left|$r = \dfrac{\ell}{1 + e\cos\theta}$|
|Up|$r = \dfrac{\ell}{1 - e\sin\theta}$|
|Down|$r = \dfrac{\ell}{1 + e\sin\theta}$|

---

## 13. Rectangular Hyperbola ($a = b$)

**Standard form:** $x^2 - y^2 = a^2$, asymptotes perpendicular, $e = \sqrt{2}$.

**Rotated $45°$ form:**

$$xy = k, \quad k = \frac{a^2}{2}$$

Asymptotes along coordinate axes. Eccentricity still $e = \sqrt{2}$.

---

## 14. Curvature

At hyperbolic parametric point $t$:

$$\kappa(t) = \frac{ab}{(a^2\sinh^2 t + b^2\cosh^2 t)^{3/2}}$$

At **vertex** ($t = 0$):

$$\kappa_{\max} = \frac{a}{b^2}, \qquad \rho_{\min} = \frac{b^2}{a}$$

---

## 15. Discriminant Condition (General Conic)

$$Ax^2 + Bxy + Cy^2 + Dx + Ey + F = 0 \text{ is a hyperbola} \iff B^2 - 4AC > 0$$

---

## 16. Quick Identification from $Ax^2 - Cy^2 + \cdots = 0$

($B = 0$, $A$ and $C$ have **opposite** signs)

Complete the square in $x$ and $y$, divide to standard form. The variable under the **positive** term opens in that direction.

---

## 17. Hyperbolic Functions (for Parametrization)

$$\cosh t = \frac{e^t + e^{-t}}{2}, \quad \sinh t = \frac{e^t - e^{-t}}{2}, \quad \tanh t = \frac{\sinh t}{\cosh t}$$

$$\cosh^2 t - \sinh^2 t = 1 \quad \checkmark$$

$$\frac{d}{dt}\cosh t = \sinh t, \quad \frac{d}{dt}\sinh t = \cosh t$$
