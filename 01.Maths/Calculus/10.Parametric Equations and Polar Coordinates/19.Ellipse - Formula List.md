

> _Every formula here traces back to one identity: $a^2 = b^2 + c^2$._

---

## 1. Fundamental Relation

$$\boxed{a^2 = b^2 + c^2}$$

|Symbol|Meaning|
|---|---|
|$a$|semi-major axis ($a > b$)|
|$b$|semi-minor axis|
|$c$|center-to-focus distance|
|$e = c/a$|eccentricity, $0 \leq e < 1$|

---

## 2. Standard Forms (Center at Origin)

**Horizontal major axis** ($a > b$, foci on $x$-axis):

$$\frac{x^2}{a^2} + \frac{y^2}{b^2} = 1$$

**Vertical major axis** ($a > b$, foci on $y$-axis):

$$\frac{x^2}{b^2} + \frac{y^2}{a^2} = 1$$

**Rule:** Larger denominator $= a^2$; foci lie on the axis of the larger denominator.

---

## 3. Standard Forms (Center at $(h, k)$)

|Major Axis|Equation|
|---|---|
|Horizontal|$\dfrac{(x-h)^2}{a^2} + \dfrac{(y-k)^2}{b^2} = 1$|
|Vertical|$\dfrac{(x-h)^2}{b^2} + \dfrac{(y-k)^2}{a^2} = 1$|

---

## 4. Key Points (Horizontal Major Axis)

|Feature|Coordinates|
|---|---|
|Center|$(h,\ k)$|
|Vertices|$(h \pm a,\ k)$|
|Co-vertices|$(h,\ k \pm b)$|
|Foci|$(h \pm c,\ k)$, $c = \sqrt{a^2-b^2}$|
|Ends of latus rectum|$\left(h \pm c,\ k \pm \dfrac{b^2}{a}\right)$|
|Directrices|$x = h \pm \dfrac{a}{e} = h \pm \dfrac{a^2}{c}$|

_(For vertical: swap $x \leftrightarrow y$ roles throughout.)_

---

## 5. Eccentricity

$$e = \frac{c}{a} = \frac{\sqrt{a^2 - b^2}}{a} = \sqrt{1 - \frac{b^2}{a^2}}$$

$$c = ae, \qquad b^2 = a^2(1 - e^2), \qquad b = a\sqrt{1-e^2}$$

|$e$|Shape|
|---|---|
|$0$|Circle|
|$\to 1$|Increasingly elongated|

---

## 6. Latus Rectum

$$\text{Length of latus rectum} = \frac{2b^2}{a}$$

$$\text{Semi-latus rectum} = \ell = \frac{b^2}{a}$$

_(Chord through focus, perpendicular to major axis.)_

---

## 7. Area and Perimeter

$$\text{Area} = \pi a b$$

$$\text{Perimeter} = 4a\int_0^{\pi/2}!\sqrt{1 - e^2\sin^2\theta};d\theta = 4a,E(e)$$

**Ramanujan's approximation:**

$$P \approx \pi!\left[3(a+b) - \sqrt{(3a+b)(a+3b)}\right]$$

**Simple approximation:**

$$P \approx 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{a^2 + b^2}{2}}$$

**Bounds:**

$$2\pi b \leq P \leq 2\pi a$$

---

## 8. Tangent and Normal Lines

At point $(x_0, y_0)$ on $\dfrac{x^2}{a^2} + \dfrac{y^2}{b^2} = 1$:

$$\text{Tangent:} \quad \frac{x,x_0}{a^2} + \frac{y,y_0}{b^2} = 1$$

$$\text{Slope of tangent:} \quad m = -\frac{b^2 x_0}{a^2 y_0}$$

$$\text{Normal:} \quad \frac{a^2 x}{x_0} - \frac{b^2 y}{y_0} = a^2 - b^2$$

**Tangent with slope $m$:**

$$y = mx \pm \sqrt{a^2 m^2 + b^2}$$

_(Condition for a line $y = mx + k$ to be tangent: $k^2 = a^2m^2 + b^2$)_

---

## 9. Parametric Equations

$$x = h + a\cos t, \quad y = k + b\sin t, \quad t \in [0, 2\pi)$$

**Tangent at parameter $t$:**

$$\frac{x\cos t}{a} + \frac{y\sin t}{b} = 1$$

**Velocity:** $\dot{\mathbf{r}} = (-a\sin t,\ b\cos t)$

**Speed:** $|\dot{\mathbf{r}}| = \sqrt{a^2\sin^2 t + b^2\cos^2 t}$

---

## 10. Polar Form (Focus at Pole)

$$r = \frac{\ell}{1 - e\cos\theta}, \quad \ell = \frac{b^2}{a}$$

|$\theta$|$r$|
|---|---|
|$0$ (periapsis)|$r_{\min} = a(1-e) = a - c$|
|$\pi$ (apoapsis)|$r_{\max} = a(1+e) = a + c$|
|$\pi/2$|$r = \ell = b^2/a$|

$$r_{\min} + r_{\max} = 2a, \qquad r_{\min} \cdot r_{\max} = b^2$$

All four orientations of the directrix:

|Directrix position|Polar equation|
|---|---|
|Left ($x = -a/e$)|$r = \dfrac{\ell}{1 - e\cos\theta}$|
|Right ($x = a/e$)|$r = \dfrac{\ell}{1 + e\cos\theta}$|
|Below ($y = -a/e$)|$r = \dfrac{\ell}{1 - e\sin\theta}$|
|Above ($y = a/e$)|$r = \dfrac{\ell}{1 + e\sin\theta}$|

---

## 11. Focus-Directrix Property

For focus $F$ and corresponding directrix $\ell$:

$$\frac{|PF|}{d(P,,\ell)} = e \quad \text{for every } P \text{ on the ellipse}$$

Directrix location (horizontal major axis):

$$x = \pm\frac{a}{e} = \pm\frac{a^2}{c}$$

Distance from center to directrix: $\dfrac{a}{e} = \dfrac{a^2}{c}$

---

## 12. Focal Radii

For a point $P = (x_0, y_0)$ on the ellipse (horizontal major axis), the distances to the two foci:

$$|PF_1| = a + ex_0, \qquad |PF_2| = a - ex_0$$

$$|PF_1| + |PF_2| = 2a \quad \checkmark$$

$$|PF_1| - |PF_2| = 2ex_0$$

---

## 13. Arc Length

$$L = \int_{t_1}^{t_2}\sqrt{a^2\sin^2 t + b^2\cos^2 t};dt$$

Full perimeter: $L = 4a,E(e)$ (see §7).

Arc length in Cartesian (for $\frac{x^2}{a^2}+\frac{y^2}{b^2}=1$, upper half):

$$L = \int_{-a}^{a}\sqrt{1 + \left(\frac{dy}{dx}\right)^2},dx = \int_{-a}^{a}\frac{\sqrt{a^4 - (a^2-b^2)x^2}}{a\sqrt{a^2-x^2}},dx$$

---

## 14. Curvature

At parametric point $t$:

$$\kappa(t) = \frac{ab}{\left(a^2\sin^2 t + b^2\cos^2 t\right)^{3/2}}$$

At the **end of major axis** $(t = 0)$:

$$\kappa_{\max} = \frac{a}{b^2}, \quad \rho_{\min} = \frac{b^2}{a}$$

At the **end of minor axis** $(t = \pi/2)$:

$$\kappa_{\min} = \frac{b}{a^2}, \quad \rho_{\max} = \frac{a^2}{b}$$

_(Radius of curvature $\rho = 1/\kappa$. Tightest curve at vertices, flattest at co-vertices.)_

---

## 15. Matrix / Quadratic Form

$$\mathbf{x}^\top M,\mathbf{x} = 1, \quad M = \begin{pmatrix} 1/a^2 & 0 \ 0 & 1/b^2 \end{pmatrix}$$

For a **rotated ellipse** (angle $\phi$ from $x$-axis):

$$M = R(\phi)\begin{pmatrix}1/a^2 & 0 \ 0 & 1/b^2\end{pmatrix}R(\phi)^\top$$

General positive-definite $M$: eigenvalues $\lambda_1, \lambda_2$ give semi-axes $a = 1/\sqrt{\lambda_{\min}}$, $b = 1/\sqrt{\lambda_{\max}}$.

---

## 16. Discriminant Condition (General Conic)

$$Ax^2 + Bxy + Cy^2 + Dx + Ey + F = 0 \text{ is an ellipse} \iff B^2 - 4AC < 0$$

---

## 17. Quick Identification from $Ax^2 + Cy^2 + Dx + Ey + F = 0$

($B = 0$, both $A, C$ same sign)

Complete the square in $x$ and $y$, then divide to get standard form. Read off $a^2$, $b^2$ from denominators (larger $= a^2$).
